# LinkedIn Rules

---

## Tone

Founder-to-founder. Analytical. Calm authority.
Write like a peer sharing a pattern they noticed — not a thought leader broadcasting.
The reader is a builder or founder. They are smart. Do not talk down to them.

---

## Format

- Hook: first 2 lines max 8 words each
- Grouped paragraphs: 2-3 related sentences together
- Never single lines stacked
- Ends with a question or sharp closer
- No links in post body — put in first comment
- No bold, no markdown, no exclamation points
- Use → ▷ ► ▲ ☞ ○ ● - for structure

---

## Character Limit

Max 2100 chars. Count before delivering.
Sweet spot for engagement: 800-1500 chars.

---

## Algorithm Tips (from Diandra Escobar, 2026)

- LinkedIn is a distribution channel for B2B, not social media
- First hour engagement matters most — comments > reactions > clicks
- Dwell time (people actually reading) signals quality to the algorithm
- No external links in post body — kills reach
- Document posts (carousels) get strong reach
- Polls drive engagement but do not build authority
- Posting Tuesday-Thursday, 7-9am performs best for B2B
- Sundays also perform well — decision makers check before their week fills up
- Comments on others' posts borrow their audience
  - 30 minutes commenting before you post your own = highest leverage for early accounts
  - Comment on posts whose audience matches your ICP, not just viral posts

---

## Profile as Funnel

The post gets the click. The profile has to convert.
Treat your profile headline, about section, featured section, and CTA button as a landing page.
About section formula: Problem → Solution → Proof → CTA

---

## Content Funnel (per Diandra Escobar)

Every post serves one of four jobs:
1. Growth — broad insights, industry observations, pattern recognition, hot takes that stop scroll
2. Authority — frameworks, case studies, deep expertise, content that makes people think "they get it"
3. Conversion — lead magnets, direct offers, newsletter CTAs, moving followers to conversations
4. Personal — your point of view woven through everything, what makes you memorable

Point of view must run through all four types. Growth without POV is noise.

---

## What Works

- Personal stories with business lessons
- Contrarian takes on industry topics
- Before/after with exact numbers (no explanation needed)
- Numbered breakdowns with data inside each point
- The courage gap — most people have the expertise but won't publish it
- Data-driven insights in a founder voice

---

## What Doesn't Work

- Overly promotional content
- Generic motivational quotes
- Corporate speak without personality
- Copy-pasting structures from other platforms
- Single lines stacked with no grouping
- News reporter framing ("Company X announced...")
