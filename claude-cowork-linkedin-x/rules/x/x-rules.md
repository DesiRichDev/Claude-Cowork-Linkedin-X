# X / Twitter Rules

---

## Tone

Punchy. Reactive. The brand's own take in real time.
Reads like a thought someone had mid-build — not a news summary, not a press release.
Each line stands on its own.

---

## Format

Single post only — no threads unless explicitly requested.
Multiple short lines. Not one paragraph.

```
Cursor hit $1B.
Built by 4 people.
No ads.
Just shipped.

This is what execution looks like.
```

NOT:

```
Cursor just hit $1B ARR which is remarkable because they only have 4 people and didn't run any paid ads, they just shipped consistently.
```

---

## Character Limit

Max 280 chars including spaces and line breaks.
Count before delivering. No exceptions.

---

## Hooks That Work on X

- Strong claims with specific numbers: "$2B ARR in 90 days."
- Counterintuitive openers: "When everyone can build apps — winning gets easier."
- Direct challenge: "Most founders will ignore this."
- Contrast pairs: "Old way: 5 years. New way: 5 weeks."

---

## X-Specific Rules

- Max 1 hashtag — use sparingly, only if it genuinely adds context
- No emojis unless the brand explicitly uses them
- No links in the post body if possible — put in reply
- No threading without a strong hook in tweet 1
- Engagement in first 30 minutes matters most
- Quote tweets with added insight beat plain retweets

---

## What Works on X (from high-performing examples)

- Social proof with specific numbers: "$31K made", "100k followers", "1000+ workflows"
- Low friction offers: "Comment X and I'll DM you"
- Urgency signals: "Free for 24hrs"
- Strong action verbs: built, cracked, scraped, automated
- Outcome language: instantly, automatically, on autopilot
- Curiosity gaps: "The results will surprise you"

---

## What Doesn't Work

- Threads without a strong hook
- Pure self-promotion
- Ignoring replies and mentions
- Scheduling everything with no real-time presence
- Long sentences that don't break
- Being too humble: "this might help" sounds weak

---

## CTA Formula for X

1. Clear action — Comment / Follow / Like
2. Specific trigger — Comment "WORD"
3. Benefit reminder — and I'll DM the full guide
4. Requirements — (must be following)
